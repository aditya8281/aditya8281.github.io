# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Multi-audience portfolio site serving:
- **Recruiters & hiring managers** evaluating Aditya for full-time roles or internships; they need fast signal on skills, project depth, and technical fit within seconds of landing.
- **Academic collaborators** (professors, research groups, potential co-authors) assessing research capability and project rigor.
- **Peer engineers & open-source contributors** looking for interesting projects, collaboration opportunities, or technical references.
- All audiences share one need: quickly understand what Aditya builds and how deeply.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio showcasing Aditya Rathod's work across edge AI, computer vision, reinforcement learning, embedded systems, and peer-to-peer networking. The site is the calling card — it must signal technical depth, real-world project execution, and engineering confidence without asking the visitor to dig. Success: a recruiter or collaborator leaves in under 90 seconds knowing what Aditya builds, what stack he uses, and that the work is real and measurable.

## Brand Personality

**Sharp. Technical. Confident.**

The site speaks like an engineer who lets the work do the talking. No hype, no decorative flourish, no visual noise. Every element earns its place through function. The tone is direct and precise — project metrics over adjectives, technical specificity over vague claims. Confidence comes from substance, not from shouting.

## Anti-references

- **Overdesigned tech bro portfolio**: neon gradients, excessive parallax, blockchain/crypto vibes, dark-mode clichés with purple-to-cyan gradients everywhere, glassmorphism used decoratively on every surface, over-the-top scroll-triggered animations that delay content, "hero-metric" templates (big number + small label + supporting stats), gradient text (`background-clip: text`), the saturated AI scaffold of tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, numbered section markers as default scaffolding (01 / 02 / 03), identical card grids with icon + heading + text.
- **Generic SaaS landing page**: cream/sand/beige backgrounds, warm-neutral tinted body bg, glass cards everywhere, gradient text, the hero-metric template.
- **Minimalist plain resume site**: just text on white with no personality, could be anyone's portfolio.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn your place.** Every visual element must justify its existence through function. If it doesn't help a recruiter, collaborator, or peer understand the work faster, remove it.
2. **Show the engineering.** Technical specificity beats vague claims. Metrics, stack details, real constraints, measurable outcomes — the site should feel like it was built by someone who ships.
3. **Depth over decoration.** The dark, atmospheric visual system should create focus and seriousness, not distract with ornamental effects. Depth through restraint, not through layering effects.
4. **Confidence through precision.** Tight typography, deliberate spacing, purposeful color use. The site should feel sharp and intentional, not approximate.
5. **Respect the visitor's time.** Fast load, instant information hierarchy, no animation delay before content. First impression in under 3 seconds.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance target (4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text)
- `prefers-reduced-motion` fully respected — all animations disabled, scroll behavior set to auto
- Keyboard navigable with visible focus indicators
- Semantic HTML structure with proper heading hierarchy
- Alt text on profile images
- Scroll-lock behavior managed for modals (resume PDF, project details)
- Mobile responsive across all breakpoints
